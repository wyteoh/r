    • Food Donation Laws

  • Bill Emerson Good Samaritan Food Donation Act: This federal law protects donors from liability when donating food in good faith to nonprofit organizations, provided the food meets quality and labeling standards.
  • State Regulations: Individual states may have additional laws and tax incentives to encourage food donation.
  • Food Donation Guidelines: The EU provides guidelines to facilitate food donation across member states, focusing on food safety, liability, and fiscal incentives.

    • Safe Handling Practices for Donating Food

    Food Safety Guidelines:
  • Temperature Control: Keep perishable foods at or below 41°F (5°C) to prevent bacterial growth.

  • Proper Labeling: Label donated foods with the name of the food, preparation date, allergen information, and storage instructions.

  • Hygiene Practices: Ensure all individuals handling food maintain proper hygiene, including regular handwashing and using clean utensils.
    • Transportation and Storage
  • Avoid Cross-Contamination: Store donated foods separately from chemicals and allergens.

  • Timely Distribution: Deliver perishable items promptly to maintain safety and quality.

    • Tools & Apps

  • Apps for Tracking Food Waste:
    • NoWaste: This app allows users to log and monitor the shelf life of their groceries, helping to minimize waste by reminding them of upcoming expiration dates.

    Kitche: Kitche helps users manage their groceries by tracking purchased items, providing reminders for use-by dates, and suggesting recipes to utilize ingredients before they spoil.
  • Platforms Connecting Donors with Food Recovery Organizations:
    • Too Good To Go: This app connects consumers with restaurants and stores offering surplus unsold food at discounted prices, reducing food waste and providing affordable meals.
    FoodCloud: Operating in the UK and Ireland, FoodCloud connects supermarkets and other food businesses with charities and community groups to donate surplus food, ensuring it reaches those who need it most.
